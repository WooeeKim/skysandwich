About

Become a Sky Sandwich

Location: Behind N25

This project began with a simple observation: although the sky is always above us, many people rarely stop to look at it.

I installed several adhesive mirror tiles in an underused space behind N25. By reflecting the sky onto the ground, the mirrors allow visitors to see the sky while looking down. Standing between the real sky above and the reflected sky below, a person briefly becomes a "sky sandwich."

The intervention was intentionally small, inexpensive, and easy to overlook. Rather than creating a large spectacle, I wanted to introduce a subtle gesture into everyday movementέΑΦa moment of pause in a place that people normally walk past without noticing.

Originally, I planned to create a continuous mirrored surface. After receiving feedback, I instead left spaces between the mirror tiles so that people could still sit, rest, and use the site naturally. This made the installation feel less like an object and more like part of the environment.

I also initially considered attaching a QR code and explicitly asking visitors to share their experiences on Instagram. In the end, I chose not to. I wanted the work to approach people indirectly rather than explain itself. Instead of instructing visitors what to do or how to feel, I hoped they would encounter the mirrors by chance and discover the experience on their own.

Documentation

Before

The site was rarely used. Most people simply passed through the area, and there was little reason to stop, sit, or look around.

During

The mirrors were installed and observed over several days. Interestingly, people showed curiosity even during the installation process itself. Some stopped to ask questions about the project, creating unexpected conversations before the work was even completed.

The installation process was documented through photographs and video recordings. A short reel documenting the intervention is currently being edited.

After

The mirrors made the location noticeably more visible, partly because they reflected sunlight throughout the day. From a distance, I observed people slowing down, looking at the reflections, checking their appearance, or simply becoming curious about the unusual surface.

The reflected sky proved more engaging than expected, particularly during sunset and on cloudy days. While the original intention was to encourage people to notice the sky, the prototype revealed another layer of interaction: visitors often saw both themselves and the sky in the same reflection.

Materials

  • Adhesive mirror tiles
  • Label paper
  • Cleaning cloth
  • Smartphone camera for documentation
